## Design Context

### Users
Factory operators monitoring a live sensor fleet, maintenance engineers validating incident evidence, and technical reviewers evaluating safe decision behavior. They need rapid situational awareness, trustworthy evidence, and direct runtime controls during a continuously updating stream.

### Brand Personality
Industrial, evidence-led, and calm under pressure. The interface should communicate operational confidence and restrained urgency without looking playful, futuristic, or like a generic analytics template.

### Aesthetic Direction
Preserve the established dark, low-glare factory-control-room aesthetic: tinted charcoal and green neutrals, restrained safety amber/red, compact technical labels, strong information density, and clear state differentiation. Maintain the existing Manrope, Space Grotesk, and DM Mono typography. Avoid decorative gradients, glow effects, glassmorphism, and visual changes unrelated to operational functionality.

### Design Principles
- Make live connection and data freshness unmistakable.
- Show evidence and policy state before interpretation.
- Reserve strong color for actionable severity and system status.
- Keep high-frequency sensor updates stable and readable without layout movement.
- Expose advanced policy controls progressively and label them as runtime-only.
- Preserve usable controls and critical status information on narrow screens.
